1. Weekly Comparison of Prices

Creating a new data frame

It can be reasonable to look at the weeks of July to see how much prices are in in each five weeks. Then, it will be possible to interpret the relationship between difference value PTF-SMF and the week number.

For this purpose, Week numbers are created using the date-time object Tarih and the data is grouped by Week to get weekly price values.

weekly_prices<-energy_prices%>%
  select(Tarih,PTF,SMF,SMF.YÃ¶n,
         Negatif.Dengesizlik.FiyatÄ±..TL.MWh.,
         Pozitif.Dengesizlik.FiyatÄ±..TL.MWh.)%>%
  mutate(Week=as.factor(week(Tarih)))%>%
  pivot_longer(.,cols = c(PTF,SMF,
                          Pozitif.Dengesizlik.FiyatÄ±..TL.MWh.,
                          Negatif.Dengesizlik.FiyatÄ±..TL.MWh.), 
               names_to = "names", 
               values_to = "values") %>%
  group_by(Week,names)%>%
  summarise(sum_price=sum(values),mean_price=mean(values))%>%
  transform(Week=as.numeric(Week))

3. Hourly Track of Average Prices in a Day

Creating new data frame

Seeing the hourly track of prices during a day will help further comment on the energy market. So, the price data are grouped by Hour and summarized by finding hourly average values.

mean_by_hour<-energy_prices%>%
  select(PTF,SMF,Tarih,SMF.YÃ¶n,
         Negatif.Dengesizlik.FiyatÄ±..TL.MWh.,
         Pozitif.Dengesizlik.FiyatÄ±..TL.MWh.)%>%
  mutate(Hour=hour(Tarih))%>%
  group_by(Hour)%>%
  summarise(mean(PTF),mean(SMF),
            mean(Negatif.Dengesizlik.FiyatÄ±..TL.MWh.),
            mean(Pozitif.Dengesizlik.FiyatÄ±..TL.MWh.))%>%

         mean_pozitifdengesizlikfiyatÄ±='mean(Pozitif.Dengesizlik.FiyatÄ±..TL.MWh.)',
         mean_negatifdengesizlikfiyatÄ±='mean(Negatif.Dengesizlik.FiyatÄ±..TL.MWh.)')

Data Visualization

ggplot(mean_by_hour) +

From the second line plot, it is clear that the patterns of the lines are pretty similar but with different price values. They both see the lowest values after 4 am like PTF and SMF do in the previous plot.

4. Relationship between Price Difference and Energy Balance

Creating new data frame

A new data.frame is needed to see the relationship between whether the difference value PTF-SMF is positive or negative, and the SMF.YÃ¶n.

difference<-energy_prices%>%

         Negatif.Dengesizlik.FiyatÄ±..TL.MWh.,
         Pozitif.Dengesizlik.FiyatÄ±..TL.MWh.)%>%
  mutate(diff=PTF-SMF)

Data Visualization

ggplot(difference) + 
  geom_area(aes(y = diff,x=Tarih, color=SMF.YÃ¶n, fill=SMF.YÃ¶n))+
  labs(x="Date", y=" Values of (PTF-SMF)")

It is seen that color of all the positive values of PTF-SMF is blue, which means âEnergy Surplusâ. Similarly, color of all the negative values is green, which means âEnergy Deficitâ. Therefore the plot confirms that if PTF is greater than SMF, actual demand is lower than predicted demand. If the situation is opposite, it is called âEnergy Deficitâ.
